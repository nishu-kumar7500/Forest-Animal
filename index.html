Indian Temples Information

Kedarnath Temple
  • Location: Uttarakhand, India, in the Himalayas
  • Built: Original structure attributed to Pandavas (c.3100 BCE), present temple built in the 8th century by Adi Shankaracharya
  • Who Built: Pandavas (legend), Adi Shankaracharya (current temple)
  • How It Is Created: The temple is made of massive stone slabs, assembled without mortar, exhibiting ancient Himalayan architecture. It stands at 11,755ft, surrounded by scenic peaks. The lingam within is a unique hump-shaped rock, symbolizing Lord Shiva.
  • Special Details: The temple survived many natural disasters and glaciation, signifying its remarkable engineering. It has mythological importance, as Lord Shiva is believed to have manifested here for the Pandavas[1][3].
Mahakal Temple
  • Location: Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh, India
  • Built: Earliest references to 4th century BCE; present structure dates to 6th century AD by Kumarasena, with major reconstructions in the 12th and 18th centuries
  • Who Built: Kumarasena (son of king Chandpradyot), rebuilt by Kings Udayaditya, Naravarman, and Maratha commander Ranoji Shinde[6][9]
  • How It Is Created: The temple exhibits classic North Indian architecture with a grand shikhar (spire), massive stone walls, and intricate carvings. The idol faces south, unique among the Jyotirlingas.
  • Special Details: Mahakaleshwar is renowned for its Bhasma aarti and as both a Jyotirlinga and Shakti Peeth[16]. It survived various destruction phases in medieval periods yet remains a center of Hindu faith.
Amarnath Guffa (Cave)
  • Location: Jammu & Kashmir, India, at 3,888m altitude
  • Built: The cave has been worshipped since antiquity; written references date to approx. 300 BCE and later[7][29]
  • Who Built: Attributed in legend to discovery by Maharishi Bhrigu and rediscovered by Buta Malik (folk legend)
  • How It Is Created: Natural cave formed in the Himalayas, containing an ice stalagmite (Shiva Lingam) created by freezing of water drops. Two smaller stalagmites are thought to represent Parvati and Ganesha.
  • Special Details: The lingam's size changes with the moon phases. The cave is open for pilgrims only in summer and is a major site for the Amarnath Yatra. It is also a Shakti Peeth[7][29].
Shree Ram Temple (Ayodhya)
  • Location: Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh, India
  • Built: Originally 11th century, reconstructed over centuries; present grand temple inaugurated in January 2024.
  • Who Built: Original structure by devotees of Lord Ram; demolished and rebuilt over centuries. Present temple constructed by Shree Ram Janmbhoomi Teerth Kshetra Trust following Supreme Court verdict in 2019[11][12].
  • How It Is Created: The temple uses traditional North Indian stone architecture with massive pillars, intricate carvings, and a huge main sanctum. It spans 360ft length, 235ft width, and uses pink sandstone.
  • Special Details: The temple stands at Ram Janmabhoomi (birthplace of Lord Ram) and symbolizes both spiritual devotion and centuries-long cultural struggles. Millions visit each year for darshan of Lord Ram[11][12][14].
